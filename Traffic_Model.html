Introduction

What is traffic jam? Why we need to care about it? What we care about?

Have you ever been trapped in a traffic jam? Have you ever passed a traffic jam and found that there is no traffic accident or other obviously reason that might induce traffic jams? Congratulation! You are trapped in one of the most interesting physical phenomenon called phantom traffic jam. This kind of jamming transition is fascinating since you can just ask some driver drive in a circular track, and, surprisingly, a jam is formed in this system. See the video below.

In order to understand this phantom traffic jam, various models have been proposed. Some describe a traffic system as a fluid, while some apply particle-based model to illustrate. One classical model describe drivers as interacting particles. Each driver tries to drive with an optimal velocity. Such velocity might depend on the velocity of drivers, the relative velocity between drivers and the vehicle in front, or the inter-vehicle distance. The simplest one is assuming that drivers decide their velocity only by the inter-vehicle distance. That is, for \(i-\)th driver, the velocity \(v_i(t)\) at time \(t\) is $$v_i(t) = v_{\text{opt}}(\Delta x_i)$$, where \(\Delta x_i = x_{i+1}-x_{i}\). However, since we are human, it is impractical to change the velocity of a vehicle with out any reaction time. Therefore, by considering this reaction time \(\tau\), the velocity of a driver should be $$v_i(t+\tau) = v_{\text{opt}}(\Delta x_i)$$. By applying Taylor expansion, the dynamical equation of each driver can be determined as $$ \frac{d v_i}{dt} = \frac{v-v_{\text{opt}}(\Delta x_i)}{\tau} $$.

So far, we did not specify the optimal velocity any function. However, there are some criteria that an optima velocity should satisfy:

  1. Since it is impossible to drive with an infinite speed even when there is no vehicles in front. Thus, optimal velocity must saturate as inter-vehicle distance approaches to infinity.
  2. Since a driver must stop when the inter-vehicle distance is zero. Thus, optimal velocity must be zero as inter-vehicle is zero.

In 1995, Prof. Bando employed a shifted hyperbolic tangent to describe optimal velocity.
