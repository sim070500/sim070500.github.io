Research Focus

My research focus on the pattern formation in active systems. The main difference between active systems and traditional passive systems are the motility of particles in systems. In recent years, lots of researches show that motility is a key factor in generating clusters. An active system can form clusters even though all particles repel each other in the system. Such kind of phase transition is so called "Motility induce phase seperation", which is a kind of density phase transition. Another interesting topic in active system is alignment phase transition. Many researches found that particles tend to align with each other in biosystems, such as E-coli systems. To generate this, iostropic symmetry breaking is neccessary. Therefore, some microscopic alignment is introduced. However, the minimize work to spontaneously generate density and alignment phase transition is still an open question.

Another interesting topic I am curious about is statistical properties in chaotic system. One crucial property of chaotic system is its undeterministic. We can not determine the future of a chaotic system by current information. However, the dynamics in chaotic system is deterministic. Futhermore, the dimension of a chaotic system can be only larger than three, which is different from the undeterministic in statistical mechanics. What is the difference between them? Is it possible to handle chaotic motion by statistical mechanics like theory? This might be anthor research I tend to conduct.
