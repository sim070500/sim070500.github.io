My Research focus

Description of my research focus

My research focus on the pattern formation in active systems. The main difference between active systems and traditional passive systems are the motility of particles in systems. The vedio shown on the right hand side is the simulation of an active system. All particles propel itself with constant velocity. The dirction of the proportional speed is influenced by a random noise. Also, all particles repel each other in this system. Interesingly, some clusters forms in this system, which is impossible when particles don't propel theselves. Such kind of phase transition is so called "Motility induce phase seperation". I am intersting in using the minimize work to generate this phase transition, and the interaction between clusters.

Other interesting topics I am curious about are statistical properties in chaotic system and pattern formations.

Tumor Morphology

Tumor Morphology

Tumor morphology serves as an important prognostic indicator for the transformation from a benign tumor to a malignant tumor. To understand the mechanism of morphological instability on the surface of tumor surface, various models have been constructed. However, a linear growth rate is alway employed to simplify calculation. In contrast, empirical data have shown that the growth rate of tumors saturates in a nutrient rich environment. By taking this factor into account, we construct a nutrient-regulated reaction-diffusion model to describe the evolution pattern of avascular tumors and the distribution of surrounding nutrients. Our model successfully reproduces the nutrient-deficiency induced instability, which has been observed before. Moreover, our model predicts that a tumor should be stable in a nutrient rich environment. Future clinical studies might be able to verify the existence of the newly discovered instability in tumor morphology.

Traffic Model

Traffic Model

The physics of traffic systems have attracted interest from scientists since 20th century since it provides a way to analyze active systems. Moreover, the analysis might shed light on the phase transition in active systems since under some conditions, a homogeneous free flow traffic might turn out to be a traffic jam. In a traffic system, some traffic jams occur with an apparent source, such as traffic accident, while some occur without any obvious reason. To understand such "phantom traffic jam", various models have been developed. In our research, we applied a classical car-following model, which is proposed by Bando in 1995, and introduced individual difference to illustrate human nature. Surprisingly, for a high density traffic system, we analytically and numerically showed that jamming formation can be suppressed in the existence of individual difference. This suggest that self-driving cars should take this factor into account
